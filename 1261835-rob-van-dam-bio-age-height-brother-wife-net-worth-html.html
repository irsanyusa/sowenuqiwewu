Rob Van Dam background

Professional wrestling

In 1990, Szatkowski entered the ring for the first time as a professional wrestler. The following year, he got his nickname, Rob Van Dam. Promoter Ron Slinker gave him the nickname. It was a perfect choice because he closely resembled the famous actor, Jean-Claude Van Damme. He also had a lot of experience in martial arts.

His career started with numerous independent promotions across his home state, Michigan. He won his first championship in July 1992, that is, the SAPW Tag Team Championship. He participated in the World Junior Heavyweight Championship at the All Japan Pro Wrestling.

World Championship Wrestling

Towards the end of 1992, Szatkowski signed a contract with World Championship Wrestling. He took the name Robbie V and made his debut in the ring on the 23rd of January 1993. He won his debut match against Pat Rose. In February, he contested the World Television Championship belt where he beat Shanghai Pierce in the first round. He lost the second to Vinnie Vegas.

Extreme Championship Wrestling

At the beginning of 1996, he signed a contract with Extreme Championship Wrestling (ECW). He defeated Axl Rotten in the first match. On the 30th of March, he faced 2 Cold Scorpio in a match where the two drew.

In 1996, RVD was very popular, especially after his widely publicised rivalry with Sabu. The two faced each other in different matches. Each of them was trying to prove that they were better than the other. Later, RVD and Doug Furnas became rivals. He also became Dan Kroffat's rival. After he lost a match to Sabu, the two teamed up to face Kroffat and Furnas. They won in a match against the two.

After the win, the duo became the rivals of John Kronus, The Eliminators, and Perry Saturn. They drew in one match, but the rivalry did not end there. The duo competed for the 1997 World Tag Team Championship belt against The Eliminators. The Eliminators won. In the next ECW competition, Van Dam beat Lance Storm.
