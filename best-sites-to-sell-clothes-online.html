Best Sites to Sell Clothes Online 2024

July 16, 2024
blog

You could say I’m a secondhand addict. I’ve been buying hard-to-find pre-loved items on eBay since I was in college, and then I moved on to Poshmark when that arrived on the scene. I regularly send clean-out kits to ThredUp and use the money to shop for beloved items for my wardrobe every season. I even added secondhand shops like Amarcord Vintage and What Goes Around Comes Around when I was writing my book, The Encyclopedia of New York Fashion. The circular economy allows you to take care of waste, clear out your home, and maybe make a few bucks, too! The financial rewards of selling your clothes online can be quite substantial, especially if you have high-quality, sought-after items.

It can be overwhelming to begin selling your used items with so many resell websites available. However, each platform has its own unique features and benefits, catering to different types of sellers and buyers. Alana Isbell, the owner of My Devil’s Thrift, prefers Depop for her online sales. “I use Depop even though they take a high percentage of your sales. They are the best for vintage sellers,” she says. “Some other great platforms are Whatnot and Jamble, which are live-selling apps. You plan a date and time and go live so people can purchase items right then and there. I haven't tried Vinted, though I have heard great things from other resellers in the community.” This variety of platforms ensures that there's a perfect fit for every seller, no matter their preferences or selling style.

While you can always donate to local organizations like churches and buy nothing groups, if you're looking to make some money from items like designer handbags, vintage tees, or workwear, check out the top places to sell second-hand clothes in 2024.

Best website to sell secondhand clothes quickly: ThredUp ‍

ThredUp is an online thrift store known for selling secondhand clothing, accessories, and shoes. It has a wide assortment of items from Target and Old Navy all the way up to Louis Vuitton, and you can get rid of your gently used items easily and quickly. Sign up for ThredUp's clean-out kit — it’s the quickest and easiest way to sell secondhand. Just sign up and ask for a kit, and ThredUp will send you a pre-labeled bag or a label to print on your box. This makes it a convenient option for anyone who wants to declutter their closets quickly.

What to know:

  • Who it's good for: People who want an easy and quick process and don’t care as much about ROI
  • Effort needed: Very little
  • Brands that sell well: Target, Old Navy, Rent the Runway, Marc Jacobs, Louis Vuitton

What we like:

  • The convenience
  • The ability to use credits to buy new clothes with thousands of pieces available

What we don’t like:

  • Lower return than selling pieces individually

The bottom line:

ThredUp is an online thrift store that sells second-hand items and offers a clean-out kit to help you declutter quickly.

Best website to sell secondhand clothes for fun: Poshmark

Poshmark is a social commerce platform that connects buyers and sellers of clothing, shoes, and accessories in new or gently used condition. Poshmark’s interactive approach that encourages user engagement through comments, likes, shares, and live shows sets it apart. While Poshmark is often considered the gold standard for reselling sites, remember that to be successful, your descriptions and photos need to be of the highest quality. You'll also need to share your closet regularly to get good visibility. We love that Poshmark has flat-rate shipping up to five pounds, so shipping is a breeze.

What to know:

  • Who it's good for: People looking for the most significant return
  • Effort needed: A good amount
  • Brands that sell well: Lululemon, Gucci, Free People, Coach, Nike

What we like:

  • The return on investment
  • The flat-rate shipping label is included in every order

What we don’t like:

  • The amount of work it takes to upload and share items

The bottom line:

Poshmark is an excellent way to sell clothes online if you’re willing to do a little work.

Tips for how to sell clothes online

Matching your wardrobe with the right platform is essential for success. It's not just about getting rid of clothes but identifying the ideal audience that will appreciate the stories behind your garments. Whether you choose ThredUP's ease, Poshmark's diverse audience, or The RealReal's discerning curation, consider which platform will showcase your clothing in the best way possible. Here are three tips that can help you sell your clothes online.

1. Take clear, well-lit photos.

If you decide to sell your clothes on ThredUP or TheRealReal, you won't have to worry about taking professional-looking photos since the site's photographers will do it for you. If you choose to sell your clothes on eBay, Facebook Marketplace, or Poshmark, you'll need to pay more attention to presentation.

To ensure high-quality photos, use bright and consistent lighting with an uncluttered background. Ensure your clothes are wrinkle-free and hung against a plain, well-lit background. Take close-up shots of labels and any wear and tear. Consider taking pictures from multiple angles to provide customers with a clear and comprehensive view of the product they will be purchasing.

2. Set a realistic price.

According to the "one-third rule" rule, you should set the price of the product at around one-third of its original retail price. For instance, if you purchased a pre-owned North Face jacket for $150, then you should set the selling price at approximately $50. However, if the coat is in excellent condition, you could try selling it for a higher price of $75 to $100. Exclusive and luxury items often sell for higher than retail, so check “comps” or comparative selling prices of similar items.

3. Describe your items clearly.

To increase the visibility of your products, provide essential details such as the brand, item name, color name, size, and condition. A prime example of a precise label that can enhance the appearance of your item in search results is "Lululemon Define Jacket in Luon

Strawberry Milkshake Size 4 Excellent Condition XS S." Many trendy brands have cult followings and websites where you can look this information up, and you can also use Google Lens to do a reverse image search, too. General condition terms used for online reselling sites are: NWT (New With Tags), Like New, EUC (Excellent Used Condition), GUC (Good Used Condition) and Fair. In this example, "Light pink Lululemon Zip Up Jacket Size 4 XS Small EUC'' will help you in search engines much more than just "pink workout jacket."

How to avoid scams when selling clothes online

To avoid scams, select platforms that have stringent policies for protecting buyers and sellers and secure payment methods to avoid scams. Never take a sale off the platform, as it gives both the buyer and seller protection. If you’re selling clothes locally using Facebook Marketplace, Next Door, or Craigslist, check the profile of the individual and always meet at a public place like a police station or school. It’s recommended that community feedback be reviewed and that the safety guidelines provided for each platform be adhered to.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Is it a good idea to sell clothes online?

Selling your pre-loved clothes online can be a win-win—you can earn some extra cash while also clearing out your closet. Keep in mind that while selling your clothes online can be worth it, only clothing in high demand and excellent condition will yield the best results. Pay attention to any applicable fees and shipping rates to ensure you’re getting the best return. But doing good for the Earth and decluttering your home can be thanks enough!

How profitable is selling clothes online?

It depends on what you’re selling, what season you’re in and what fashion trends are. Parkas, puffer jackets, and jeans for men sell best in the winter months, while tank tops and sundresses do better in summer. For the best chances of selling, try to get your items on second-hand selling apps a few months before each season. To make the best profit, keep clothes in great condition and sell them when trends are hot.

Which app is best for selling clothes online?

All of our top nine sites to sell secondhand clothes are recommended due to their high customer satisfaction, but each has its strengths. Poshmark, ThredUp, and Vinted are excellent options for selling casual attire, like t-shirts for women, jeans for women and flannels for men thanks to their easy-to-use interfaces and engaged users. These platforms offer a broad selection of fashion styles and appeal to a diverse audience of potential buyers.

The RealReal and Vestiaire Collective are excellent choices if you're interested in selling premium fashion items. These platforms offer authentication services and have a customer base that is specifically looking for high-end products. Their emphasis on luxury brands helps ensure that your designer clothing will be exposed to the appropriate audience, and Poshmark and eBay verify luxury purchases over a certain price point.
