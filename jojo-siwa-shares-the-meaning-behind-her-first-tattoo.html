JoJo Siwa recently got her first tattoo and shared the meaning behind the ink on social media. As it turns out, the tattoo’s design is significant for a few reasons — one of which Siwa can’t reveal yet.

JoJo Siwa Gets First Tattoo, Shares Meaning

While getting her new tattoo, Siwa was accompanied by Raven-Symoné and her wife Miranda Maday. The 20-year-old got her first ink behind her ear. The simple design features the number “1031” parallel to the back of her ear.

Maday posted a photo of Siwa lying on her side and smiling while getting inked, writing “bbs first tattoo.” She also shared a shot of Raven-Symoné getting a new tattoo on her arm, writing that the star has “too many to count.”

“I’m obsessed,” Siwa wrote on Snapchat over a video of her showing off the tattoo. She added, “The official meaning is 1031 is how many days my first concert four lasted, but then 03 is the year that I was born so that’s why it looks like 03 with the lines on the side.”

Siwa added that three is “a very significant number but I can’t say why yet.” She included several shushing face emojis to accompany that tease. We’ll just have to wait and see what she’s referring to. Hopefully she won’t keep it a secret for long.

Siwa Called Out Candace Cameron Bure Again

Siwa recently made headlines for her appearance on Nick Viall’s podcast The Viall Files. During their conversation, Siwa once again called out Full House star Candace Cameron Bure, whom he had previously named as the rudest celebrity she’d ever met.

The two stars talked things out after Siwa made the claim, but the 20-year-old says she doesn’t regret what she said after learning about Bure’s comments on “traditional marriage” after partnering with the Great American Family channel.

“It’s fine if you’re doing it because it’s just your movie’s storyline,” Siwa told Viall. “Not everything needs to be gay, essentially. But when you’re doing it out of spite to say, ‘Too much is about LGBTQ right now, you guys suck, and I wanna make a movie about traditional marriage and you’re not traditional,’ that got to me a little bit.”
